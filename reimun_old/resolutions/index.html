Resolution-writing
This page is intended for the slightly more experienced MUN delegate, who may want to write their own resolution to submit at conference.  More experienced delegates are often expected to bring resolutions to conference on the topics they are debating.

The document below outlines the process of writing a resolution. It gives a guide to the expected structure, content and format of a resolution, as well as tips on how to write a resolution.

How to write a resolution

The following document (provided in docx, doc and pdf format) is a resolution template.  It is in the correct format, so it simply needs to replace the existing preamble and operative clauses with your own clauses and to add the title information (committee, topic and submitter name).

The example resolution given in the document above is briefer than would be expected at most conferences.  For more example resolutions, you may like to consult the booklet MUN Resolution Collection. This booklet contains some successful resolutions at conferences in previous years, either submitted by delegates from Reigate Grammar School at other conferences or by delegates from other schools at ReiMUN.  These resolutions are reproduced with permission.  Please note that these are for example only.  Delegates should not under any circumstances copy these resolutions, in part or in full, and submit them as their own resolutions.
